Welcome to Orange County!

Discovering what Orange County has to offer feels like uncovering a new side of California with every turn. You can spend the morning surfing at Huntington Beach, wander through the art galleries of Laguna in the afternoon, and end the day watching the sunset from a cliffside trail in Crystal Cove. Beyond its beaches, the county offers world-class dining, Disneyland and theme parks in Anaheim, and peaceful nature escapes. Farmers' markets, local festivals, and coastal trails invite you to slow down and explore the regions mix of creativity and community. Whether you are here for adventure, culture, or simply the sun, OC always has something waiting to surprise you!

Getting around OC is generally easy and flexible, whether you are driving or using public transit. Most areas are well connected by major freeways like the 5, 405, and Pacific Coast Highway, making it convenient to travel between beaches and inland communities. Many towns also have bike-friendly routes and walking paths that make short trips scenic and enjoyable. There are a lot of parking spots, especially near popular destinations, and signage throughout the county is clear and well-maintained. If you prefer not to drive, check OCTA.

OC parks offer a refreshing escape from the urban pace, blending ocean views and peaceful open spaces. From the coastal cliffs of Crystal Cove State Park to the canyons of Laguna Coast Wilderness Park, outdoor enthusiasts can hike or simply unwind surrounded by nature. In the Inland Empire, spots like Irvine Regional Park and Peters Canyon provide miles of trails and picnic areas great for families. Each park captures a different side of the Southern California landscape, whether it is a quiet beach walk or a sunrise trail run. For nature lovers, the OC Parks website is a great resource for exploring hiking trails, regional parks, and coastal preserves.

Food in Orange County reflects Californians love for fresh, flavorful, and convenient dining. Whether you are craving a classic In-N-Out Burger or a local favorite like The Habit Burger Grill, there is always something quick and satisfying nearby. For a taste of diverse flavors, you can find authentic taco stands, Korean BBQ spots, and poke bowl joints scattered across the county. Cities like Costa Mesa and Anaheim also feature food halls and farmers markets that showcase local produce. Orange County makes casual dining an experience as lively and varied as its communities. Top Spots

Heisler Park (Laguna Beach)

  • Palm trees
  • Ocean overlook
  • Great at sunrise

A clifftop path with art, gardens, and steps leading to small coves.

Metered and free parking nearby. Low tide reveals excellent tide pools so watch your step!

Crystal Cove State Park

  • Coastal hikes
  • Historic cottages
  • Wide beaches

Miles of shoreline and bluff trails between Newport and Laguna.

Day use fee at lots; bring water and sun protection for mid-day at the beach.

Huntington Beach Pier

  • Surf culture
  • Long pier walk
  • Food nearby

Classic SoCal pier with surfers and a great hiking trails.

Lots and garages available. Wind can pick up so layer up in the evening.

Balboa Island (Newport Beach)

  • Harbor views
  • Boardwalk stroll
  • Frozen banana ice cream

Small island with a waterfront path, ferry, and dessert shops.

Street parking can be tight weekends; try side streets or come earlier hours.

Mission San Juan Capistrano

  • Historic ruins
  • Rose gardens
  • Museum exhibits

18th-century mission complex with serene courtyards.

Admission required; check hours and events on the official site.
